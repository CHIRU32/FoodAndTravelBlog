SALMAN RUSHDIE

Sir Ahmed Salman Rushdie CH FRSL[2] born 19
June 1947 is an Indian-born British-American
novelist.[3] His work often combines magic
realism with historical fiction and primarily deals
with connections, disruptions, and migrations
between Eastern and Western civilizations, typically
set on the Indian subcontinent.Rushdie's second
novel, Midnight's Children (1981), won the Booker
Prize in 1981 and was deemed to be "the best novel
of all winners" on two occasions, marking
the 25th and the 40th anniversary of the prize.
After his fourth novel, The Satanic Verses (1988),
Rushdie became the subject of several assassination
attempts and death threats, including a fatwa calling
for his death issued by Ruhollah Khomeini, the
supreme leader of Iran. Numerous killings and
bombings have been carried out by extremists
who cite the book as motivation, sparking a
debate about censorship and religiously motivated
violence.
